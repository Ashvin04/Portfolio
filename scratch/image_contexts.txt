[P 17]  ScriptableObject (SO) Channels: These channels acts as a form of medium between broadcasters and listeners. Broadcasters and listeners will know about the ScriptableObject , but not about each other, making it completely decoupled. For example, when a player spawns/ dies, they register/ deregister with the PlayerCameraChannelSO . This allows the MultiplayerCameraManager to simply listen to this channel, without ever needing direct references to the player prefabs, and updates its cinemachine target group accordingly.
[P 18]  Local C# Actions &amp; Network Variables: Component-level communication is handled by Action delegates and the NetworkVariable callbacks. The NetworkHealth component broadcasts OnHealthChanged and OnDeath events. PlayerHUDController has these events subscribed so that the UI reacts instantly to damage without any tightly coupled logic.

[P 1]  ROUNDS (Landfall) – The game has a mechanic where players can pick a power up from a selection of “cards”. The power-up picking mechanic is similar to the ones seen in rogue-lite. Games like Hades and Dead Cells are great for a singleplayer setting, however these games are expected to be part of a long tun, so by design, the power ups essentially drip-feed the player with buffs. ROUNDS is a multiplayer game designed to be played in quick bursts, making players have less power-ups overall. ROUNDS combats this by making the power-ups “mechanic changing”. Essentially one power-up can completely change the way you play your game. For example, there is a power-up that transforms their base pistol into a shotgun like weapon. This changes the play style from being able to shoot at any distance to needing to have to be in close quarters but having the advantage of extremely high damage. This shows how the design philosophy between a singleplayer and a multiplayer game differs greatly even when you compare similar mechanics. Games like Call of Duty rewards players for performing well with game changing abilities via the ScoreStreak/ Killstreaks system. I have, however, found that players tend to gain a sense of frustration when the opposing team is dominating, and then they gain access to helicopters, essentially rendering them unstoppable/ ROUNDS’s power up mechanic acts more like a catchup mechanic . Players who are winning are not only challenged by the enemies getting harder as they win, it also gives losers a sense of “correction” when being able to gain a power-up to counter-act the winning players strength. One shortcoming of this game however is that there are moments where players can enter a slate-mate . This image shows the 2 players on equal footing. The game has no means of forcing an end (e.g. no timer, no hazards etc etc). This could cause camping, which slows down the pacing of a round, ruining the flow of the fast-paced game. This is definitely an area where they can improve . CITATION Lan21 \l 2057 (Landfall, 2021)
[P 2]  Fortnite (Epic Games) - Fortnite Battle Royale (will be called Fortnite for short) is a PvP battle royale game . It is heavily reliant on players fighting other players for the gameplay loop (however has been limited time events with interactable NPCs in a match). Fortnite boasts a large playable area. If we look at Call of Duty Black Ops Cold War, there is a game mode within the game called Outbreak. It, too, boasts a large map, however it is purely a PvE experience that can be taken on by yourself or with friends. These 2 games show how a singleplayer game and multiplayer games have significant differences in how these large-scale maps work. In outbreak, the developers are able to create a sense of constant action. As players, traverse the map to explore points of interest, the game can throw hordes of zombies, mini – bosses, and large-scale events to keep the player on their toes. Fortnite is heavily reliant on player interactions. This means that the developers can’t throw hordes of enemies to keep a player engaged throughout the match but rather design their game in a manner where the players are forced to interact with each other. The primary way Fortnite achieves this is by use of its “Storm”. This essentially pushes players inwards, making the playable space smaller. This is essential for keeping the player engaged with constant combat scenarios , since the a match relies on perma-death, so the total amount of players in a match constantly reduces, hence needing to push players towards a central location. CITATION Epi17 \l 2057 (Epic Games, 2017)
